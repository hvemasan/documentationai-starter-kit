## What Is a Board?

A board is a container for posts. Every feedback post lives on a **feedback board**, and every roadmap item lives on a **roadmap board**. Boards let you separate concerns — for example, a "Mobile App" board distinct from a "Web Dashboard" board — and apply different visibility, voting, and access rules to each.

You can create as many boards as you need. There's no hard limit.

## Two Board Types

| Type | Used For | Where It Appears |
|------|----------|------------------|
| **Feedback Board** | Customer-submitted ideas, problems, requests | Feedback tab on the public portal |
| **Roadmap Board** | Internal planning — what you're building, in what order | Roadmap tab on the public portal |

## Create a Board

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open board settings" icon="clipboard-list">
    Go to **Settings → Feedback** (titled **Feedback Boards**) — or **Settings → Roadmap** for a roadmap board — and click **Add Board**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name and describe" icon="type">
    Give your board:

    - **Icon & Name** — an optional small image plus the name customers will see (e.g., "Mobile App Feedback")
    - **Description** *(optional)* — a short blurb describing what the board is for

    The board's URL slug is generated from the name automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set who can see posts" icon="eye">
    Under **Who can see posts**, choose the board's visibility:

    - **Public** — anyone with the link
    - **Internal** — only signed-in team members
    - **Segmented Users** — only end-users matching a [segment](/setup/user-segments) you choose
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set who can create posts" icon="pencil">
    On feedback boards, **Who can create posts** is controlled independently of visibility:

    - **Public** — anyone
    - **Internal Team** — only your team members
    - **Segmented Users** — only end-users matching a segment you choose
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure board options" icon="sliders">
    Turn features on or off per board:

    - **Disable Voting** — turn on to stop users upvoting posts (voting is enabled by default)
    - **Disable Commenting** — turn on to stop users discussing posts (commenting is enabled by default)
    - **Display Post Dates** — show when posts were submitted
    - **Show ETA** *(roadmap boards only)* — display estimated completion dates

    <Callout kind="alert">
      The Voting and Commenting toggles are phrased as **Disable Voting** and **Disable Commenting**. Leaving them **off** keeps the feature **enabled** (the default); turning them **on** disables the feature. Easy to flip in your head — double-check before saving.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save" icon="check">
    Your board is live immediately at `your-org.productbridge.io/feedback/<board-slug>` (or `/roadmap/<board-slug>` for roadmap boards).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## When to Use Multiple Boards

A single board is fine for most teams starting out. Consider splitting into multiple when:

- **You have multiple products** — separate "Acme Pro" and "Acme Lite" boards keep feedback streams distinct
- **You have multiple audiences** — a "Beta Customers" board with segment visibility, plus a public board for everyone else
- **Volume is overwhelming** — splitting "Feature Requests" from "Bug Reports" into different boards keeps each focused
- **Different teams own different areas** — Mobile team owns the Mobile board, Web team owns the Web board

<Callout kind="tip">
  Don't over-split early. It's easier to add a second board later than to merge two boards back into one. Start with one, see where the conversation gets crowded, then split.
</Callout>

## Board-Level Custom Fields

Each **feedback board** controls which [custom fields](/setup/custom-fields) appear on its post form. For example, your "Enterprise Feedback" board might collect an "Account ID" field that your public board doesn't need.

Configure these in the board form under **Customize post input fields**. This section appears on feedback boards only — roadmap boards don't have custom fields.

## Visibility & Access Rules

Visibility and post-creation access are **independent** — you can have a board that's publicly visible but only lets your team or a specific segment post, or vice versa.

| Who Can See Posts | Who Can Create Posts | Common Use Case |
|-------------------|----------------------|------------------|
| Public | Public | Standard public feedback hub |
| Public | Internal Team | Public roadmap-style board your team curates |
| Public | Segmented Users | Publicly readable, but only customers can request features |
| Internal | Internal Team | Team-only ideation board |
| Segmented Users | Segmented Users | Beta board scoped to your Beta Customers |

See [Visibility & SEO](/settings/visibility-and-seo) for the full breakdown of visibility levels.
