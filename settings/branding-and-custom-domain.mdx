## Make ProductBridge Look Like Your Product

When customers land on your public portal, you want it to feel like an extension of your product — not a third-party tool. ProductBridge lets you control branding (colors, favicon) and map your own domain (e.g., `feedback.yourproduct.com`) so the entire experience stays on-brand.

## Branding

Branding settings apply across your public portal, customer-facing emails, and embedded widgets.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Branding settings" icon="palette">
    Go to **Settings → General → Branding**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your brand color" icon="droplet">
    Pick a primary brand color. This is the accent color used for buttons, links, and highlights across your portal.

    You can configure **two variants** — one for light mode and one for dark mode — so your portal looks great in either theme.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload a favicon" icon="image">
    Upload your product's favicon (the small icon shown in browser tabs). PNG or ICO formats work best.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save" icon="check">
    Save your changes. Branding updates apply instantly across the portal — no redeploy needed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Setting | Description | Where It Appears |
|---------|-------------|------------------|
| Brand color (light) | Hex color used in light theme | Portal buttons, links, accents |
| Brand color (dark) | Hex color used in dark theme | Portal buttons, links, accents |
| Favicon | Browser tab icon | Public portal, email templates |

<Callout kind="tip">
  Pick brand colors with enough contrast against both white and dark backgrounds. If your brand color is very light, set a darker variant for dark mode so it stays readable.
</Callout>

## Custom Domain

By default, your portal lives at `your-org.productbridge.io`. With a custom domain, you can serve it from a subdomain you own — for example, `feedback.yourproduct.com` or `ideas.yourproduct.com`.

### How It Works

ProductBridge uses **Cloudflare for SaaS** under the hood to provision SSL certificates and route traffic to your custom domain. You add a DNS record at your domain registrar, and ProductBridge handles the rest — certificate issuance, renewal, and edge routing.

### Add Your Custom Domain

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Domain settings" icon="globe">
    Go to **Settings → General → Custom Domain** and toggle **Enable Custom Domain**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your domain" icon="link">
    Enter the hostname you want to use, for example `feedback.yourproduct.com`.

    <Callout kind="info">
      Use a valid hostname only — no `https://`, no trailing slash, no port, no path. ProductBridge validates the format before saving.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add the DNS record" icon="server">
    After saving, ProductBridge displays a **verification token** and **ACME TXT record** (name and value). Add both to your DNS provider:

    - A `CNAME` record pointing your hostname to the target ProductBridge provides
    - A `TXT` record using the ACME name and value (used by Cloudflare to issue your SSL certificate)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for verification" icon="clock">
    DNS propagation typically takes a few minutes but can take up to 24 hours. ProductBridge polls verification status automatically — refresh the settings page to see when your domain switches from **Pending** to **Verified**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Done" icon="check">
    Once verified, your portal is live at your custom domain with a valid SSL certificate. The default `*.productbridge.io` URL continues to work as a fallback.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Verification Status

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Pending | DNS records not yet detected, or certificate still being issued |
| Verified | Domain is live and serving traffic with a valid SSL certificate |

<Callout kind="alert">
  Don't delete the DNS records once verified — Cloudflare uses them to renew your SSL certificate automatically. Removing them will eventually cause your domain to stop working.
</Callout>

### Troubleshooting

<ExpandableGroup>
  <Expandable title="My domain is stuck on Pending">
    Verify that:

    - The `CNAME` record points to the exact target ProductBridge gave you
    - The `TXT` record name and value match exactly (these are case-sensitive)
    - DNS propagation has had time to complete — use a tool like [dnschecker.org](https://dnschecker.org) to confirm your records are visible globally

    If everything looks right and the status is still Pending after a few hours, contact support.
  </Expandable>
  <Expandable title="I get a certificate warning in the browser">
    This usually means the SSL certificate hasn't been issued yet. Wait 10–15 minutes after the domain shows **Verified** and try again. Certificate issuance happens asynchronously after verification.
  </Expandable>
  <Expandable title="Can I use an apex domain (yourproduct.com)?">
    We recommend using a subdomain like `feedback.yourproduct.com`. Apex domains often have DNS limitations that prevent `CNAME` records, which can complicate setup. If you need apex support, contact us.
  </Expandable>
</ExpandableGroup>
