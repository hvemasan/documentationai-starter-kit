## Send Email From Your Own Domain

By default, ProductBridge sends emails through our shared system mailer. To send from your own domain, connect your own SMTP server (Google Workspace, Amazon SES, Postmark, SendGrid, Mailgun, etc.) under **Custom SMTP**.

<Callout kind="info">
  Custom SMTP is never required for delivery. Every ProductBridge email falls back to the system mailer when custom SMTP isn't configured or fails — nothing is skipped.
</Callout>

## Custom SMTP

Use this option to route ProductBridge emails through your own SMTP server. Choose this if you already have a mail infrastructure and want everything to flow through it.

<Callout kind="info">
  Custom SMTP is available on plans that include the `email_notifications` feature.
</Callout>

### Set Up Custom SMTP

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open SMTP settings" icon="server">
    Go to **Settings → Brand Settings**, open the **SMTP** tab, and turn on the **Custom SMTP** switch.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your SMTP credentials" icon="key">
    Provide:

    - **Sender Name** — the display name shown in the inbox (e.g., "Acme Product Team")
    - **Email Address** — the address messages are sent from (e.g., `feedback@yourproduct.com`)
    - **SMTP Host** — your SMTP server hostname (e.g., `smtp.gmail.com`, `email-smtp.us-east-1.amazonaws.com`, `smtp.postmarkapp.com`)
    - **Port** — typically `587` (TLS) or `465` (SSL)
    - **Username** — your SMTP login
    - **Password** — your SMTP password or app-specific token

    <Callout kind="alert">
      Your password is encrypted at rest and only decrypted at send time. We never log or display it after you save.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test the connection" icon="zap">
    Click **Test Connection**. ProductBridge attempts a real SMTP handshake using the credentials you provided.

    On success, the status changes to **Active**. On failure, you'll get an error so you can fix the credentials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Done" icon="check">
    Once active, outgoing email from ProductBridge routes through your SMTP server.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Status

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Not Tested** | Credentials saved but the connection test hasn't run yet |
| **Active** | Test connection succeeded — your SMTP is used for outbound email |
| **Error** | Last test failed — emails fall back to the system mailer until fixed |

You can re-test or update credentials at any time.

## How Delivery Works

ProductBridge sends every email using a two-tier strategy:

1. **Your custom SMTP** — tried first when configured and active
2. **System SMTP fallback** — used if custom SMTP isn't configured or fails

A misconfigured SMTP setup won't block delivery — every email type falls back to the system mailer until you fix it.

## Which Emails Are Affected?

When your custom SMTP is active, **all outbound emails** from ProductBridge route through it:

- [Changelog subscriber announcements](/notifications/email)
- [Post assignment notifications](/notifications/email)
- [Status change and comment emails](/notifications/email)
- Team member invitations
- Subscription confirmation emails
- Any other transactional message

## Common Provider Setups

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Google Workspace" icon="mail">
    - **SMTP Host:** `smtp.gmail.com`
    - **Port:** `587`
    - **Username:** the full email address (e.g., `feedback@yourproduct.com`)
    - **Password:** an [App Password](https://support.google.com/accounts/answer/185833) generated for your Google Account — your normal Google password will not work

    <Callout kind="alert">
      You must enable 2-Step Verification on the Google Account to create App Passwords.
    </Callout>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Amazon SES" icon="server">
    - **SMTP Host:** `email-smtp.<region>.amazonaws.com` (e.g., `email-smtp.us-east-1.amazonaws.com`)
    - **Port:** `587`
    - **Username & Password:** SMTP credentials generated in the SES console (not your AWS access keys)
    - **Verify your sender** in SES before testing — SES rejects sends from unverified addresses
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Postmark" icon="send">
    - **SMTP Host:** `smtp.postmarkapp.com`
    - **Port:** `587`
    - **Username:** your Postmark Server Token
    - **Password:** the same Server Token (Postmark uses it for both)
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="SendGrid" icon="cloud">
    - **SMTP Host:** `smtp.sendgrid.net`
    - **Port:** `587`
    - **Username:** `apikey` (literally the string `apikey`)
    - **Password:** your SendGrid API Key with at least "Mail Send" permission
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Troubleshooting

<ExpandableGroup>
  <Expandable title="SMTP test fails with authentication error">
    - Double-check the username and password — try logging into your provider's webmail or sending from another tool with the same credentials
    - For Gmail, you must generate an **App Password** under your Google Account security settings — your normal password won't work
    - For Amazon SES, use the SMTP credentials from the SES console, not your AWS IAM access keys
    - For SendGrid, the username is the literal string `apikey` (not your account email)
  </Expandable>
  <Expandable title="SMTP test fails with connection timeout">
    - Verify the hostname is correct and reachable from the public internet
    - Check that the port is allowed by your provider — `587` (TLS) is most common; `465` (SSL) and `25` (plain) are alternatives
    - Some networks block outbound SMTP — try a different port or confirm with your provider
  </Expandable>
  <Expandable title="Test passes but customers report not receiving emails">
    - Check the recipient's spam folder
    - Confirm your sending domain has proper **SPF**, **DKIM**, and **DMARC** records — most major providers reject mail without them
    - Look at your SMTP provider's delivery logs for bounces or rejections
    - Make sure your sender email is verified with your provider (especially for SES and similar)
  </Expandable>
  <Expandable title="My emails are going out but not from my domain">
    - This usually means custom SMTP is in **Not Tested** or **Error** status, so ProductBridge is falling back to the system mailer. Click **Test Connection** again
    - Confirm the **Email Address** field matches a verified sender on your SMTP provider — providers reject mail with mismatched From addresses
  </Expandable>
</ExpandableGroup>
