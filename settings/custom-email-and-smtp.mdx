## Why Configure Custom SMTP?

By default, ProductBridge sends emails through our shared system mailer. That works fine for getting started, but most teams eventually want to:

- Have customers see emails coming from `feedback@yourproduct.com` instead of a generic sender
- Maintain consistent sender reputation tied to your own domain
- Get full visibility into delivery via your own SMTP provider's logs and analytics
- Comply with internal policy that all outbound mail flows through approved infrastructure

Connecting your own SMTP server makes ProductBridge use **your mail infrastructure** for every outbound email — assignments, changelog announcements, roadmap status updates, the lot.

## How It Works

ProductBridge sends emails using a two-tier strategy:

1. **Your custom SMTP** — if configured and active, every outbound email is sent through it
2. **System SMTP fallback** — if your custom SMTP isn't configured, or a send through it fails, ProductBridge falls back to our system mailer so the email still gets through

This means a misconfigured custom SMTP won't block your customers from receiving critical mail — it just won't come from your domain until you fix the issue.

<Callout kind="info">
  Some email flows require custom SMTP to be active. [Assignment emails](/notifications/email) and roadmap status change emails skip silently if custom SMTP isn't configured. Changelog announcements always send via the system mailer if needed.
</Callout>

## Set Up Custom SMTP

You'll need SMTP credentials from your mail provider. Popular options include Google Workspace, Amazon SES, Postmark, SendGrid, Mailgun, and Microsoft 365.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open SMTP settings" icon="server">
    Go to **Settings → Email → Custom SMTP** and toggle **Enable Custom SMTP**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your SMTP credentials" icon="key">
    Provide:

    - **Sender email** — the address messages are sent from (e.g., `feedback@yourproduct.com`)
    - **Sender name** — the display name shown in the inbox (e.g., "Acme Product Team")
    - **Host** — your SMTP server hostname (e.g., `smtp.gmail.com`, `email-smtp.us-east-1.amazonaws.com`, `smtp.postmarkapp.com`)
    - **Port** — typically `587` (TLS) or `465` (SSL)
    - **Username** — your SMTP login
    - **Password** — your SMTP password or app-specific token

    <Callout kind="alert">
      Your password is encrypted at rest and only decrypted at send time. We never log or display it after you save.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test the connection" icon="zap">
    Click **Test Connection**. ProductBridge attempts a real SMTP handshake using the credentials you provided.

    On success, the status switches to **Active**. On failure, you'll get the specific error (auth failed, host unreachable, TLS handshake failed, etc.) so you can fix it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Done" icon="check">
    Once active, every outgoing email from ProductBridge routes through your SMTP server.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Status

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Pending** | Credentials saved but the connection test hasn't yet succeeded |
| **Active** | Test connection succeeded — SMTP is in use for every outbound email |

You can re-test or update credentials at any time. ProductBridge automatically reverts to **Pending** if you change credentials, so a fresh test is required before sends resume.

## Which Emails Use Your SMTP?

When your SMTP is active, **all** outbound emails from ProductBridge route through it:

- [Changelog subscriber announcements](/notifications/email)
- [Post assignment notifications](/notifications/email)
- [Roadmap status change emails](/notifications/email)
- Team member invitations
- Subscription confirmation emails
- Any other transactional message

## Common Provider Setups

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Google Workspace" icon="mail">
    - **Host:** `smtp.gmail.com`
    - **Port:** `587`
    - **Username:** the full email address (e.g., `feedback@yourproduct.com`)
    - **Password:** an [App Password](https://support.google.com/accounts/answer/185833) generated for your Google Account — your normal Google password will not work

    <Callout kind="alert">
      You must enable 2-Step Verification on the Google Account to create App Passwords.
    </Callout>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Amazon SES" icon="server">
    - **Host:** `email-smtp.<region>.amazonaws.com` (e.g., `email-smtp.us-east-1.amazonaws.com`)
    - **Port:** `587`
    - **Username & Password:** SMTP credentials generated in the SES console (not your AWS access keys)
    - **Verify your sender** in SES before testing — SES rejects sends from unverified addresses
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Postmark" icon="send">
    - **Host:** `smtp.postmarkapp.com`
    - **Port:** `587`
    - **Username:** your Postmark Server Token
    - **Password:** the same Server Token (Postmark uses it for both)
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="SendGrid" icon="cloud">
    - **Host:** `smtp.sendgrid.net`
    - **Port:** `587`
    - **Username:** `apikey` (literally the string `apikey`)
    - **Password:** your SendGrid API Key with at least "Mail Send" permission
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Troubleshooting

<ExpandableGroup>
  <Expandable title="Authentication failed on test">
    - Double-check the username and password — try logging into your provider's webmail or sending a test from another tool with the same credentials
    - For Gmail, you must generate an **App Password** under your Google Account security settings — your normal password won't work
    - For Amazon SES, use the SMTP credentials from the SES console, not your AWS IAM access keys
    - For SendGrid, the username is the literal string `apikey` (not your account email)
  </Expandable>
  <Expandable title="Connection timed out or host unreachable">
    - Verify the hostname is correct and reachable from the public internet
    - Check that the port is allowed by your provider — `587` (TLS) is most common; `465` (SSL) and `25` (plain) are alternatives
    - Some networks block outbound SMTP — try a different port or confirm with your provider
  </Expandable>
  <Expandable title="Test passes but customers report not receiving emails">
    - Check the recipient's spam folder
    - Confirm your sending domain has proper **SPF**, **DKIM**, and **DMARC** records — most major providers reject mail without them
    - Look at your SMTP provider's delivery logs for bounces or rejections
    - Make sure your sender email is verified with your provider (especially for SES and similar)
  </Expandable>
  <Expandable title="My emails are going out but not from my domain">
    - This usually means custom SMTP is in **Pending** status, so ProductBridge is falling back to the system mailer. Click **Test Connection** again to move it to **Active**.
    - Confirm the **Sender email** field matches a verified sender on your SMTP provider — providers reject mail with mismatched From addresses
  </Expandable>
</ExpandableGroup>
