## Two Ways to Brand Your Outbound Email

By default, ProductBridge sends emails through our shared system mailer. To send from your own domain, you have two options:

| Option | When to Use |
|--------|-------------|
| **Custom Sending Domain** | Easiest setup — register a subdomain and verify it via DNS. Recommended for most teams. |
| **Custom SMTP** | Use your own SMTP server (Gmail Workspace, AWS SES, Postmark, etc.). Choose this if you need full control over the sending infrastructure or have an internal policy requiring it. |

You can configure either — or both. If both are configured, ProductBridge prefers your custom SMTP and falls back to the system mailer when needed.

<Callout kind="info">
  Some email flows have stricter requirements. [Assignment emails](/notifications/email) and roadmap status change emails skip silently if custom SMTP isn't configured. Changelog announcements always send via the system mailer if needed.
</Callout>

## Custom Sending Domain

Use this option to send emails from a subdomain you own (e.g., `notifications.yourdomain.com`) without running your own SMTP server. You register the subdomain in ProductBridge, add a few DNS records, and we handle delivery.

### Set Up Your Sending Domain

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Email Domain settings" icon="mail">
    Go to **Brand Settings → Email Domain** and click **Add Domain**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter a subdomain" icon="at-sign">
    The domain must be a **subdomain** with at least three parts — for example:

    - ✅ `notifications.yourproduct.com`
    - ✅ `mail.yourproduct.com`
    - ❌ `yourproduct.com` (apex domain — not allowed)

    Using a subdomain keeps ProductBridge sends isolated from your primary email infrastructure.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add the DNS records" icon="server">
    After registration, ProductBridge displays the DNS records to add to your DNS provider. These typically include records for authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC) and mail routing.

    Add each record exactly as shown — name, type, and value are all case-sensitive.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify" icon="check-circle">
    Once your records are in place, return to **Brand Settings → Email Domain** and click **Verify**. ProductBridge checks each record and updates the verification status.

    DNS propagation can take anywhere from a few minutes to a few hours. If verification fails, double-check the record name and value (some DNS providers truncate long values).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure your sender" icon="user">
    Once verified, set:

    - **Sender name** — the display name customers see (e.g., "Acme Product Team")
    - **Sender email** — the address emails come from (must be on your verified subdomain)
    - **Reply-to email** *(optional)* — a different address for replies (can be on any domain)
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Verification Status

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Pending** | Domain registered, DNS records not yet detected |
| **Verified** | All records confirmed — domain is sending |
| **Failed** | One or more records missing or incorrect |

You can re-check status from the Email Domain settings at any time.

### Delete a Domain

To remove a sending domain, click **Delete Domain** in the Email Domain settings. You'll be asked to confirm. After deletion, the domain is unregistered from our sending infrastructure and your outbound email reverts to the system mailer (unless you also have Custom SMTP configured).

## Custom SMTP

Use this option to route ProductBridge emails through your own SMTP server. Choose this if you already have a mail infrastructure (Google Workspace, Amazon SES, Postmark, SendGrid, Mailgun) and want everything to flow through it.

### Set Up Custom SMTP

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open SMTP settings" icon="server">
    Go to **Brand Settings → SMTP** and toggle **Enable Custom SMTP**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your SMTP credentials" icon="key">
    Provide:

    - **Sender email** — the address messages are sent from (e.g., `feedback@yourproduct.com`)
    - **Sender name** — the display name shown in the inbox (e.g., "Acme Product Team")
    - **Host** — your SMTP server hostname (e.g., `smtp.gmail.com`, `email-smtp.us-east-1.amazonaws.com`, `smtp.postmarkapp.com`)
    - **Port** — typically `587` (TLS) or `465` (SSL)
    - **Username** — your SMTP login
    - **Password** — your SMTP password or app-specific token

    <Callout kind="alert">
      Your password is encrypted at rest and only decrypted at send time. We never log or display it after you save.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test the connection" icon="zap">
    Click **Test Connection**. ProductBridge attempts a real SMTP handshake using the credentials you provided.

    On success, the status changes to **Verified**. On failure, you'll get a specific error (auth failed, host unreachable, TLS handshake failed, etc.) so you can fix it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Done" icon="check">
    Once verified, every outgoing email from ProductBridge routes through your SMTP server.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Status

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Not Tested** | Credentials saved but the connection test hasn't run yet |
| **Verified** | Test connection succeeded — SMTP is in use for every outbound email |
| **Error** | Last test failed — emails fall back to the system mailer until fixed |

You can re-test or update credentials at any time. Changing credentials resets the status to **Not Tested** until you re-run the test.

## How Delivery Works

When both options are configured, ProductBridge sends emails using a two-tier strategy:

1. **Your custom SMTP** — tried first when status is **Verified**
2. **System SMTP fallback** — used if custom SMTP isn't configured or fails

A misconfigured Custom Sending Domain or SMTP won't block delivery — it just falls back to the system mailer until you fix it.

## Which Emails Are Affected?

When your custom configuration is active, **all outbound emails** from ProductBridge route through it:

- [Changelog subscriber announcements](/notifications/email)
- [Post assignment notifications](/notifications/email)
- [Roadmap status change emails](/notifications/email)
- Team member invitations
- Subscription confirmation emails
- Any other transactional message

## Common Provider Setups (Custom SMTP)

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Google Workspace" icon="mail">
    - **Host:** `smtp.gmail.com`
    - **Port:** `587`
    - **Username:** the full email address (e.g., `feedback@yourproduct.com`)
    - **Password:** an [App Password](https://support.google.com/accounts/answer/185833) generated for your Google Account — your normal Google password will not work

    <Callout kind="alert">
      You must enable 2-Step Verification on the Google Account to create App Passwords.
    </Callout>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Amazon SES" icon="server">
    - **Host:** `email-smtp.<region>.amazonaws.com` (e.g., `email-smtp.us-east-1.amazonaws.com`)
    - **Port:** `587`
    - **Username & Password:** SMTP credentials generated in the SES console (not your AWS access keys)
    - **Verify your sender** in SES before testing — SES rejects sends from unverified addresses
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Postmark" icon="send">
    - **Host:** `smtp.postmarkapp.com`
    - **Port:** `587`
    - **Username:** your Postmark Server Token
    - **Password:** the same Server Token (Postmark uses it for both)
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="SendGrid" icon="cloud">
    - **Host:** `smtp.sendgrid.net`
    - **Port:** `587`
    - **Username:** `apikey` (literally the string `apikey`)
    - **Password:** your SendGrid API Key with at least "Mail Send" permission
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Troubleshooting

<ExpandableGroup>
  <Expandable title="Sending Domain verification stays Pending">
    - DNS propagation typically takes minutes but can take hours — wait and re-check
    - Confirm every record name and value matches exactly (case-sensitive)
    - Some DNS providers truncate long `TXT` values — make sure the full value is saved
    - Make sure you registered a **subdomain** (e.g., `notifications.yourdomain.com`), not an apex domain
  </Expandable>
  <Expandable title="SMTP test fails with authentication error">
    - Double-check the username and password — try logging into your provider's webmail or sending from another tool with the same credentials
    - For Gmail, you must generate an **App Password** under your Google Account security settings — your normal password won't work
    - For Amazon SES, use the SMTP credentials from the SES console, not your AWS IAM access keys
    - For SendGrid, the username is the literal string `apikey` (not your account email)
  </Expandable>
  <Expandable title="SMTP test fails with connection timeout">
    - Verify the hostname is correct and reachable from the public internet
    - Check that the port is allowed by your provider — `587` (TLS) is most common; `465` (SSL) and `25` (plain) are alternatives
    - Some networks block outbound SMTP — try a different port or confirm with your provider
  </Expandable>
  <Expandable title="Test passes but customers report not receiving emails">
    - Check the recipient's spam folder
    - Confirm your sending domain has proper **SPF**, **DKIM**, and **DMARC** records — most major providers reject mail without them
    - Look at your SMTP provider's delivery logs for bounces or rejections
    - Make sure your sender email is verified with your provider (especially for SES and similar)
  </Expandable>
  <Expandable title="My emails are going out but not from my domain">
    - This usually means custom SMTP is in **Not Tested** or **Error** status, so ProductBridge is falling back to the system mailer. Click **Test Connection** again
    - Confirm the **Sender email** field matches a verified sender on your SMTP provider — providers reject mail with mismatched From addresses
  </Expandable>
</ExpandableGroup>
