## Talk to Your Feedback Data

Ask AI lets you query your entire feedback dataset using plain English. Instead of building filters, exporting spreadsheets, or writing SQL, you type a question and get an answer in seconds.

Ask AI understands context, handles follow-up questions, and can summarize, compare, and quantify feedback across any dimension.

## How to Use Ask AI

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Ask AI" icon="sparkles">
    Navigate to your project and click **Ask AI** in the sidebar, or use the keyboard shortcut `Cmd+K` (Mac) / `Ctrl+K` (Windows).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Type Your Question" icon="message-square">
    Enter a question in natural language. For example:

    - "What are the top 5 feature requests this month?"
    - "How has sentiment around onboarding changed over the last 90 days?"
    - "Which customer segment submits the most negative feedback?"
    - "Summarize all feedback related to mobile performance"
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the Answer" icon="check">
    Ask AI returns a structured answer with supporting data — charts, feedback excerpts, and links to the original items. You can drill down into any result or ask a follow-up question to refine your query.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Example Queries

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Trend Analysis" icon="trending-up">
    **Question:** "What topics are trending up this quarter compared to last?"

    Ask AI compares category volumes across time periods and highlights topics with significant growth. You see both the percentage change and representative feedback items for each trend.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Segment Comparison" icon="users">
    **Question:** "How does feedback from enterprise customers differ from SMB customers?"

    Ask AI breaks down feedback by customer segment, comparing top categories, average sentiment, and volume. This helps you prioritize features for your most valuable segments.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Impact Assessment" icon="target">
    **Question:** "Which open feature requests would impact the most users?"

    Ask AI ranks feature requests by the number of unique users who mentioned or voted for them, weighted by recency. Use this to make data-driven prioritization decisions.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Tips for Better Results

<Callout kind="tip">
  Be specific with time ranges and segments for more precise answers. "Feedback about pricing from enterprise users in the last 30 days" returns sharper results than "what do people think about pricing?"
</Callout>

- **Use time frames** — "this month", "last quarter", "since January" help scope your query
- **Name categories** — reference specific topics like "onboarding", "billing", or "API" for focused answers
- **Ask follow-ups** — Ask AI retains context, so you can drill deeper with "break that down by plan tier" or "show me examples"
- **Request formats** — Ask for "a summary", "a list", "a comparison", or "a chart" to control the output style
