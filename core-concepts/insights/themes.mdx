## The Big Picture

**Themes** are the highest-altitude stories in your feedback — groups of related observations gathered under the functional areas of your product (for example, *Resume Editor*, *Onboarding & Education*, or *Feedback Collection*). Where an observation is one specific problem, a theme tells you which **part of the product** is generating the most discussion.

## Reading the Themes Board

Each theme is shown as a card with:

- **Name** — the functional area (for example, *Resume Editor*)
- **Status** — `Emerging`, `Active`, `Dismissed`, or `Resolved` (see [Status](/core-concepts/insights#status))
- **Summary** — a short description of what the theme covers
- **Metrics** — `observations · people · posts`: how many observations roll up into it, how many distinct people are behind it, and how many feedback posts contribute
- **Source breakdown** — how the contributing feedback splits across sources (for example, *Public 1 · Appsumo 2*)

## Filter and Sort

- **Status** — show only themes in a given status
- **Source** — keep themes with evidence from a specific source
- **Sort** — order by **Newest** or another available option

## Open a Theme

Select a theme to open it and see the **observations** grouped inside it — and from there the **evidence**, the individual signals and source feedback posts. This lets you move from "*Resume Editor* is noisy this month" down to the exact customer quotes in a couple of clicks.

<Callout kind="tip">
  Themes are deliberately broad — a healthy product usually has only a handful. If you are looking for a specific problem rather than an area, start with [Observations](/core-concepts/insights/observations).
</Callout>
