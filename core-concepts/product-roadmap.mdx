## Plan with Confidence

The Product Roadmap in ProductBridge is where insights become action. You create roadmap items, link them to real user feedback, and prioritize based on demand — not assumptions. Share your roadmap publicly to build transparency, or keep it internal for team alignment.

## Creating Roadmap Items

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add a Roadmap Item" icon="plus">
    Open **Roadmap** under **Modules** in the sidebar and click **New Item**. Enter a title, description, and select a status.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Link Feedback" icon="link">
    Attach related feedback items to the roadmap entry. You can search by keyword, category, or feedback ID. ProductBridge also suggests relevant feedback based on the item's title and description.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Prioritize" icon="arrow-up">
    Open the **Prioritization** view from the Roadmap header, pick a framework (RICE, ICE, Impact/Effort, or MoSCoW), and score the item — see [Prioritization](#prioritization) below.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Roadmap Statuses

Organize your roadmap with clear status columns that communicate progress:

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Under Review | Evaluating feasibility and demand |
| Planned | Approved and scheduled for development |
| In Progress | Actively being built |
| Completed | Shipped and available to users |

<Callout kind="tip">
  Customize status labels and add your own stages in **Settings → Modules → Statuses** (Manage Statuses) to match your team's workflow. Roadmap boards are configured under **Settings → Modules → Roadmap**.
</Callout>

## Linking Feedback to Roadmap Items

Every roadmap item can have feedback linked to it. This connection is bidirectional:

- **From the Roadmap** — Click **Link Feedback** on any item to search and attach feedback
- **From the Feedback Inbox** — Select feedback items and click **Add to Roadmap** to link them to an existing or new roadmap entry
- **From Insights** — Promote an [Observation](/core-concepts/insights/observations) to the roadmap; its supporting feedback comes with it

When a roadmap item's status changes, users who submitted linked feedback can be notified automatically — closing the feedback loop.

## Prioritization

Click the **Prioritization** button in the Roadmap header to open the prioritization table. Every roadmap item appears as a row; pick a framework, enter its parameters for each item, and ProductBridge computes the score and ranks the list.

Four frameworks are available:

| Framework | Parameters you enter | Score |
|---|---|---|
| **RICE** | Reach, Impact, Confidence (%), Effort | (Reach × Impact × Confidence) ÷ Effort |
| **ICE** | Impact, Confidence, Ease | Impact × Confidence × Ease |
| **Impact/Effort** | Impact, Effort | Impact ÷ Effort |
| **MoSCoW** | Category: Must have, Should have, Could have, Won't have | Must = 4, Should = 3, Could = 2, Won't = 1 |

You set every parameter manually, directly in the table. Items missing a required parameter score 0 and sort last. You can switch frameworks at any time — each item stores its parameters for all four, so nothing is lost when you change perspective.

<Callout kind="info">
  Priority comes from the framework, not a separate priority picker. Sort by score to see what to build next, or sort by newest, oldest, or last modified.
</Callout>

## Public and Private Roadmaps

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Public Roadmap" icon="globe">
    Share your roadmap on your public portal's **Roadmap** tab at `https://{your-subdomain}.productbridge.io/roadmap` (or on your custom domain). Users see planned features, current progress, and completed items — building trust and reducing "when will this ship?" questions.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Internal Roadmap" icon="lock">
    Keep your roadmap private for internal team use. Only workspace members with the appropriate role can view and edit roadmap items. Use this for early-stage planning before committing publicly.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
